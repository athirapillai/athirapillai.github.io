LOCALHERO



LocalHero

For a sponsored project in a Usability Engineering Course, our theme was "Citizens helping in their communities". To promote this theme, my team and I are currently working on LocalHero, an application that will allow people to easily give back to their community while winning rewards.

THE ISSUE

People often want to help out in their community and volunteer, but they tend to have trouble finding a volunteering activity related to their interests and staying motivated to continuously volunteer.

Several applications already exist that are devoted toward networking volunteering activities, but many of these applications only use a potential user’s want to help as incentive for using the service. This sadly does not tend to attract more users to an application that can benefit from as many users as possible.

On the other hand, local charities and charitable organizations need as much as help as they can get for their events, but it's difficult finding people to help with each and every event.

THE SOLUTION

Our team will create an aesthetically pleasing prototype of the LocalHero application that provides an easy, positive experience to our users.


- LocalHero will have an aspect of gamification and a reward system to help bring in more users that would like to help.
- This application will allow charitable organizations to publish events to a public platform. Users, dubbed Heroes, will have the ability to view a list of events occurring in their area and accept events in which they would like to partake.
- When a Hero reaches a certain amount of points, he/she can receive coupons or gift cards as they level up from sponsoring companies, such as Target or Walmart.

DESIGN STANDARDS


LocalHero: ITERATION 1


First Step: Research



Research Questions:

  • What other similar applications exist that would be our competition?
    Hypothesis: There are many applications out there that offer connections to volunteers, including apps like GiveGab, Golden, Volunteer Finder, and One Today, but we will go beyond the competition with the incentive for users to continuously complete tasks.

  • What types of sponsors would assist in providing rewards for greater levels?
    Hypothesis: There would be coupons, discount codes, or gift cards for local or franchise businesses, possibly broken up by area code or state.

  • What are other ways to push users to reusing the application to complete more tasks?
    Hypothesis: The application would update or refresh rewards periodically to keep users interested in the application. Levelling up will also make them a more well-rounded “Local Hero”.

  • What is our application’s key demographic?
    Hypothesis: Our application’s demographic would focus on more socially involved users, probably younger and more active, more involved in volunteering, but also those wanting to volunteer with some kind of small reward for helping.

  • Does this key demographic require a simpler interface?
    Hypothesis: The demographic for this application would likely not find trouble with most existing interfaces, but for users that are very young, a bit of simplification of the application would be necessary.

  • How would you prevent someone from falsely gaining points?
    Hypothesis: We would have some form of vetting process, perhaps conducted by the organizations we work with, for people putting up events so that we would not have someone dishonestly farming points by making fake events and saying that they completed them.
